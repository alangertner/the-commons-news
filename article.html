The CUSMA Carveout Is Meaningless If Small Businesses Can't Navigate It

Ottawa needs to move fast — not with diplomacy, but with practical help for Canadian exporters right now.

By The Commons February 22, 2026
Why It Matters

Trump's new 10% global tariff technically exempts CUSMA-compliant Canadian goods. But compliance requires documentation that many small and mid-size exporters aren't equipped to produce. The Canadian Chamber of Commerce is calling for urgent federal guidance — and so far, Ottawa's response has been diplomatic statements, not practical help.

The Details
  • The White House fact sheet confirms CUSMA-compliant goods are exempt from the new 10% global tariff announced this week
  • But exporters must prove compliance at the border with detailed rules-of-origin documentation — a complex legal and accounting exercise
  • Large manufacturers with dedicated trade counsel will manage. Small businesses with 10 to 50 employees — the backbone of Canadian exports — will struggle
  • The Canadian Chamber of Commerce issued an urgent call for Ottawa to fast-track compliance guidance, simplified documentation templates, and a dedicated hotline
  • Meanwhile, the U.S. Supreme Court's Friday ruling striking down Trump's emergency tariff powers creates additional uncertainty. Markets reopen Monday into fog
  • The Canadian dollar closed at 71.2 cents U.S. on Friday. Analysts expect volatility across the TSX, particularly in export-heavy sectors

The Bottom Line

A tariff exemption you can't access isn't an exemption at all. The Carney government needs to deliver practical help — compliance hotlines, simplified documentation, expedited guidance — not just diplomatic statements from the PMO.
